White Hat Hackers

White hat hackers, also known as ethical hackers, use their skills to protect organizations from cyber threats. They conduct penetration testing and security assessments to identify and fix vulnerabilities before malicious hackers exploit them. Companies, governments, and cybersecurity firms rely on white hat hackers to strengthen their digital defenses. Their work is legal and crucial in safeguarding sensitive information and maintaining cybersecurity.

Roles of White Hat Hackers:

  • Conduct penetration testing to find security flaws.
  • Help organizations strengthen their cybersecurity measures.
  • Work with governments and businesses to prevent cyber attacks.
  • Educate and train others in ethical hacking practices.
